Robust Quantitative Model of the Drosophila Wing Pouch

A contribution of the WingJ project is to enable the integration of phenotypic and expression data collected from many individual systems. The goal is to obtain a single and reliable multiscale model generated from multiple "snapshots" of the system studied. We envision that such quantitative models will be very useful in scientific research, in particular for reverse engineering predictive multiscale models of biological organisms.

The interface below enable to visualize a quantitative model of the developing Drosophila wing. This model can be used to gain insights into the domains of expression of different gene products in wild type and pentagone deficient wings at different time points during development. The gene products are respectively associated to the red, green and blue image channels to produce RGB images.

For each experimental condition, the shape of the wing has been generated from the individual structure of fifteen to thirty wings. The dashed line represents the standard deviation of the structure models. The domain of expression of each gene product corresponds to a mean expression map computed from five to ten individual wings. Both the individual and combined snapshots have been generated using WingJ.
